Did you manage not to stumble on a dramatic post explaining how someone generated 10,000 lines of code with AI while wasting time on your LinkedIn feed? Congratulations, you're lucky.

However, as Nathaniel Fishel explained in his [Your Code Is Worthless](https://nathanielfishel.substack.com/p/your-code-is-worthless) article, the "lines of code" is a useless vanity metric that sounds great in a LinkedIn self-promotion, but doesn't matter when one has to maintain the product one has shipped to the customers. Add the natural laziness, and you have a perfect storm. As he wrote:

> Evolution taught us that cognitive energy is a scarce resource. If we can avoid thinking deeply about a complex architectural trade-off by pressing a “magic solve it for me” button, we usually will.

Some people (including some managers) understand that; others (as in previous hype cycles) are bound to learn the lesson the hard way unless they're fortunate enough to change jobs before the skyscraper of cards collapses.
