Here's a short glimpse into the history of telecommunications: in a building at the top of this mountain (barely noticeable blip across the saddle from the radio tower; search for Capo Figari for more details), [Guglielmo Marconi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guglielmo_Marconi) conducted experiments in the ~1930s (after inventing the _wireless telegraph system_ in the late 1890s).

{{<figure src="/2026/05/marconi.jpg">}}

The original radio could "transmit" at most 40-60 words per minute (the limit of a [skilled Morse Code operator](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Morse_code#Operator_proficiency)). 130 years later, I'm writing this blog post using a 200 Mbps Internet connection via a low-earth-orbit satellite with response times low enough that I can run an interactive SSH session with no noticeable delay. It's almost incomprehensible how far we've come in such a short time.
