I always wanted to find someone who is more positive about AI than I am, while having solid "can deliver working stuff at scale" credentials. [Andrew Yourtchenko](https://www.linkedin.com/in/ayourtch/) definitely fits the bill.  I first met him (online) when he was still an engineer in Cisco TAC, and when we finally met in person, he was busy automating the [deployment of Cisco Live networking infrastructure](https://blog.ipspace.net/2019/10/how-do-you-provision-500-switch-network/). He was also instrumental in bringing us closer to ubiquitous IPv6 deployment with [Happy Eyeballs](https://datatracker.ietf.org/doc/html/rfc6555).

After reading his LinkedIn posts, I thought he was all-in on AI, but it turned out our views are mostly aligned—we're both very cautious optimists, well aware of many of the new tools' shortcomings. For more details, listen to [Episode 207](http://media.blubrry.com/ipspace/stream.ipspace.net/nuggets/podcast/Show_207-AI_in_Networking_with_Andrew_Yourtchenko.mp3) of [Software Gone Wild](https://www.ipspace.net/Podcast/Software_Gone_Wild/).
