Following a link in [Martin Fowler](https://martinfowler.com/)'s [Fragments](https://martinfowler.com/fragments/2026-05-05.html), I stumbled upon [Genie Tarpit](https://tidyfirst.substack.com/p/genie-tarpit) by Kent Beck -- a perfect summary of my experiences with AI coding (code reviews are OK, new code less so). He also provided a good reason for that behavior:

> The “plausible deniability” task orientation of the genie leaves it claiming success even though the code doesn’t work at all.

And the proposed solution?

> You probably saw this one coming—nobody knows.

Yeah, that about sums it up.
