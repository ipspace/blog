--- url: /2006/10/running-syslog-over-tcp/ title: "Running syslog over TCP" date: "2006-10-20T11:48:00.000+02:00" tags: [ syslog,logging ] --- Syslog has always been considered an undependable means of reporting network problems by serious network administrators as it runs over unreliable UDP transport. Sometime in the twilight zone between IOS releases 12.3T and 12.4, Cisco IOS got the capability to transport syslog messages over TCP with the logging host ip-address transport tcp port configuration command (the command is documented in 12.4 manuals but missing in 12.3T manuals).

Note: IOS implements standard syslog stream over TCP, not the more complex RFC 3195.

To support syslog over TCP, you also need TCP-capable syslog server. In Unix environments, you can use syslog-ng, on Windows, Kiwi syslog daemon is a perfect choice.

Note: to enable syslog over TCP in Kiwi Syslog Daemon, go to File/Setup/Inputs/TCP, click Listen for TCP Syslog messages and enter the desired TCP port number.
