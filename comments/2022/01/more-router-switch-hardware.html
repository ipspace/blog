    FYI: The three things that I look for when answering this question are:

    1) A big enough FIB to hold a global BGP table (v4 + v6) - most "data center" switch ASICs don't meet this requirement (e.g., XGS) though Dune/DNX does. 2) Hierarchical queuing (e.g., queue1... queue4 feed into queue5) 3) Deeper buffers; if you assume[1] you need "bandwidth time delay product" worth of buffers, a 400Gbps data center chip (assume rtt<1ms) needs 100x less buffer than a WAN 400Gbps WAN interface with a Rtt=~100ms interface.

    [1] Though a lot of very smart people have argued that the bandwidth delay product is too large... that's a different discussion.
