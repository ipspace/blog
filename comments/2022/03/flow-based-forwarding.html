    First I'd like to add a little bit of information regarding the Enterasys S-Series and K-Series:

    The K-Series, mentioned in the link given by Ivan above, was a smaller, cheaper offspring of the S-Series for use as access switches. The S-Series was positioned for use as core and distribution switches.

    To provide an impression for readers who might not know these Enterasys devices, but have some familiarity with Cisco Catalyst switches: where an Enterasys marketer would have placed an S-Series, a Cisco marketer might have placed a Catalyst 6500; where an Enterasys marketer would have placed a K-Series, a Cisco marketer might have placed a Catalyst 4500.

    Then I'd like to support Ivan's remain skeptical position:

    Whenever marketing slides position a device as generally better than everything else, because it uses Technology X®™ (patent pending), this might not be completely accurate.

    Using a topology-based forwarding router with too small hardware tables works less well than using a flow-based router with sufficient performance. The difference here is not the forwarding technology used, but selecting a device fitting the requirements.

    Topology-based packet forwarding enables the use of addressing hierarchy to scale a deployment. Sadly I have seen networks where the selected topology-based devices did not work well, because the so called network "design" did not include such an addressing hierarchy, and the devices used would have required it. Surprisingly, in some of those networks, replacing the selected chassis switches with Enterasys S-Series would have solved the performance problems without a re-design. This, of course, is nothing more than an anecdote.

    I think these Enterays switches provide an interesting example, because they used a technology different from the mainstream, and they did generally work well.

    [Enterasys Networks was acquired by Extreme Networks in 2013, and most former Enterasys customers have now either replaced their flow-based switches with topology-based ones or have started the process of doing so.]
