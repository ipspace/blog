    How will IPv6 help (or hinder) the flooding of BUM frames? With IPv6 using ND multicast frames instead of ARP broadcasts, won't this help to some degree, similarly to VXLAN flooding over IP multicast?
    Replies
    1. Ivan Pepelnjak 15 May 2012 08:06
      IPv6 uses L2 multicast instead of L2 broadcast. Multicast and broadcast frames are flooded in the same way (unless the switches use IGMP and/or MLD snooping, in which case they can limit IPv4 and/or IPv6 multicast flooding), and the impact on hypervisors would be the same.

      To get full benefits of IPv6 ND, you need switches with MLD snooping.
