    I can give you an old example... :-)

    Novell NCP originally trusted that after the initial authentication, whatever is in the header of the subsequent NCP packets can be trusted.

    Then there were some hacks, and they introduced digital signatures into NCP. This way they got ZTA. Just the marketing term was not invented yet.

    They had a nice directory service behind all of their services that supported those preparation steps.

    So Novell had this already decades ago... :-)

    However, what I see now that vendor presentations describe such overly complicated architectures that would cause more trouble than benefits.

    So as usual, use your own common sense judgement and make a cost-benefit analysis.

    And of course, technology is useless without proper processes executed correctly by educated and well motivated people. The weakest link, as always, is still the uncaring employee...
