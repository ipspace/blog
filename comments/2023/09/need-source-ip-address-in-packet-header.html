    I once heard someone talking about SMTP say "I really wish they would have called it 'Return Address' and not 'From' because this might help relate it to postal services where you can put whatever return address you want on an envelope and drop it off anywhere. It's not telling you where a piece of mail came from, it's telling you how to reply and the two may not be related".

    I'm paraphrasing of course, but I think the same analogy can be made about IP to a large extent. IP Networks can/should add in things like urpf or bcp38, but they typically don't come with that out of the box because IP makes very little assumptions about the topology of the network. It's up to the operator to design that and implement spoofing protection (which is why it often does not get done).

    Of course the analogy breaks down at some point, but it's a nice way to stop assuming src address is where the thing actually came from if you want to think about security
