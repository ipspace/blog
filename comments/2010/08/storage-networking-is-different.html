    You also need to consider what happens when a peak race condition occurs. For example, a burst of backup traffic combined with a virus scan at the same time that critical service runs can cause major networking problems where queues can be overrun, switch fabrics overloaded, and more.

    Classic FC copes by using an XON/XOFF method combined with over specification of the network, while IP queueing and buffering handles iSCSI/NFS methods. Two methods to solve the same problem.
