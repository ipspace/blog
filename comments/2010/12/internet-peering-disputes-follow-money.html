    Well this is what you get in datagram networks with distributed control :) No end-to-end QoS, no end-to-end management (includes accountability), nothing end-to-end in fact. In turn, this effect is a large-scale reflection of the hop-by-hop independence principle introduced by packet networks. This has been one of the main challenges to "transport neutrality" for years - if X sends an *unsolicited* packet to Y across A,B,C and D, how would A,B,C and D bill X and Y for such transportation? If X and Y are paying, do they have any control over the path their packets take? And while nothing much we could do about it in existing architecture we can always blog about the good, the bad and the ugly! :)
