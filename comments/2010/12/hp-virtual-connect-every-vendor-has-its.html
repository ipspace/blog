  6. Tschokko 22 December 2010 16:49
    Why do you compare VC with a network switch? In my opinion it's a virtualization layer between the servers and the network. It makes the management of the blade system much easier and since you can virtualize MAC and WWN addresses/ids you're not bound to specific component. You can even move servers from one chassis to another. If you use SAN boot, you can start the machine every you want, like a virtual machines. Lot's of benefits, even if VC is only a "dumbed down" switch. But the primary focus of VC is not switch functionality, it's virtualization!
