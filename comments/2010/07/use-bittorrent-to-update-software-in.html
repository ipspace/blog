    Well the whole idea of reliable multicast was relying on NAKs (negative ACKs) as opposed to positive ACKs - this reduces the upstream feedback flow - and implementing intermediate buffering - this improves local stream recovery. The design was therefore relatively scalable, but never reached widespread adoption, though there is (limited) support in Cisco IOS.
