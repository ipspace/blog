  3. Enno Rey 10 September 2014 19:14
    Hi Ivan,

    thanks! for the - as usual - quite enlightening post.
    May I add some unordered remarks?

    You write about "run a pilot project" and "do an audit before deploying IPv6".
    Not sure if the problem(s) would have been discovered/exposed in a pilot as they seem related to "coupling effects" (see RFC 3439) once a certain scale is/as reached, which in turn "the pilot" might not have (had).
    As for the audits you mention ... given the two of us are regularly involved in enterprise projects - how many of such audits have you seen in IPv6 space? Me, to be diplomatic ;-), well: not too many.

    Further you state: "Also, if you don’t use IPv6 multicast, I don't see any need to run MLD on the LAN – turn it off if you can".
    That's exactly (part of) the problem: to the best of my knowledge you simple can't (turn it off), at least not on the host/OS level. which is why we did our testing described in http://www.insinuator.net/2014/09/mld-and-neighbor-discovery-are-they-related/ by locally blocking outgoing MLD packets (which of course is not a strategy for large scale operations. we did it just for testing purposes).

    Best

    Enno
