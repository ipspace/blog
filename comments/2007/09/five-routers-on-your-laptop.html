    Never tried it. A single router running OSPF and BGP (plus a zillion other processes IOS starts by default) consumes around 20% of CPU (40% of one core), so my eight routers consume 100% of both cores. That's not a real problem, as long as the routers can respond to routing protocol packets (and they are still OK in my scenario, no dropped adjacencies).

    I have no idea what they want you to start in the 13-router lab, but if there are many concurrent features being configured, you might get over the edge.
