    Openflow (at least 1.0) probably best compares to Tcl, with C++ being noteworthy for its heavyweight complexity ;) In my opinion, the main selling point for Openflow in large-scale (100k+ servers) data-centers is significant simplification (if not complete reduction) of network control plane. Complex, multi-vendor software is the main barrier on the way to unified automation and improved network stability. In a highly-specialized networks, such as simple symmetric full-bisection bandwidth topologies, feature richness of common control/management plane stacks becomes unnecessary, more likely turning into source of software bugs and operational headache. On the "innovation" side, being praised by Openflow adepts, large scale networks would probably mostly benefit from advanced, automated, troubleshooting tools not found in modern IP networks.
