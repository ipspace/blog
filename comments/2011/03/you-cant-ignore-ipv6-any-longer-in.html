  12. Ivan Pepelnjak 24 March 2011 20:04
    The problem they had then was NAT (as a principle, not NAT44 in particular). In the meantime, (some) app developers have realized it's not a good idea to carry IP addresses in application messages without some additional mechanisms.

    There's even a standard way to build NAT-traversing applications (STUN) and NAT64 was developed by the same working group and should work well with STUN. Any well-written application should thus work as easily over NAT64 as it does over NAT44 ... which brings us to another problem: many applications (anything browsers-based obviously excluded) simply haven't realized (yet) that there's a new address family you have to take in account.

    I don't have any first-hand information about large-scale NAT64 trials. Juniper does have stateful NAT64 working on one of their edge routers (http://www.juniper.net/techpubs/en_US/junos10.4/information-products/topic-collections/nce/nat64-ipv6-ipv4-depletion/configuring-nat64-ipv6-ipv4-depletion.pdf)
