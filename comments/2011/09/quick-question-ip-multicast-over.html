    Years ago I stuffed a multicast stream into a GRE tunnel with some success. The multicast was a unidirectional IP video stream pulled from a satellite. The streams source IP was an RFC-1918 that I couldn't route on our multicast enabled backbone, so I NAT'd the stream to a routeable addresses using a PIX prior to sending outbound from the site.

    Unfortunately, the source had a TTL of 4, and the broadcast TV guys that uplinked the stream didn't know how to change that, so I stuffed the stream into GRE tunnels before crossing the backbone., just to keep the TTL from decrementing.
