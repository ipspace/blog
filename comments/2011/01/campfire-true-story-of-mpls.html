    Yep, spot on Ivan, this absolutely matches my memory. I first came across tag switching/MPLS in around the '97-'99 timeframe. As I remember it, tag switching was originally meant to help with performance: instead of a router having to perform a classical IP lookup, it would instead just do a simple tag lookup in order to determine the egress interface (and tag). As you say, in those early days, it was nothing at all to do with VPNs -- that came a bit later on, along with all the other derived technologies that complicate our lives today (and keep us busy in work!).

    As I remember it, at around the turn of the millennium, the performace gains that tag switching/MPLS were meant to provide were largely negated by the advent of wire-speed routers (e.g. Juniper M40/M160, Cisco GSR) along with higher-speed WAN interfaces (e.g. STM-64 PoS). This was all during the dot-boom and before the crash, so it was happy days! 8-)
