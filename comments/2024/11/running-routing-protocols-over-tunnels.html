    Great article.
    For Ivan's Type 3 VPN setup, I always use two different VRFs.

    Global VRF: This is the underlay with general Internet. Frequently I have one static route for 0.0.0.0/0.

    Internal VRF: This is the customer network, frequently OSPF.

    With VRFs I never have to dance around filtering routes into and out of the OSPF. In fact, nothing fancy needs to be done at all. Clean separation every time.

    I did my first infinite recursion when doing IP (with IPX/SPX) over GRE over IPSec. Never fun. Don't want to troubleshoot that ever again.
