  5. Anonymous 25 June 2015 22:47
    The TOR is no longer an "access" switch. Today's high-density, high-speed pizza-box/non-modular switches deployed at the TOR serve as aggregation switches (with access relegated to the soft-switch inside the virtualized server). If you think HA is critical in the legacy aggregation/core switch, then this new class of aggregation points in the current data-centers will need them in the enterprise still running legacy apps (now virtualized, of course). Juniper, Cisco and Brocade all support this on their high-density fixed-form factor TOR switches they target for enterprise DCs.
