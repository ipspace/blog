    2. Anders 04 May 2015 16:26
      I remember a similar issue with the server-side email filtering language SIEVE: in its early days, it used 2000/tcp by default, but later did move to 4190/tcp. 2000/tcp is still in use by legacy configurations, but later became assigned to Cisco's Skinny protocol. As a result, an ASA in place may accept the connection (client thinks "server accepts the tcp connection), the ASA doesn't read skinny protocol packets and hence closes the connection (clients thinks "server closed connection"). Of course, the SIEVE server doesn't ever see an incoming connection from that client - and so one can easily spend days debugging this.
