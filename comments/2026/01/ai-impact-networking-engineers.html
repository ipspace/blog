    Well, transistor as a switch, switches as NAND gates, NAND gates as logic ICs, logic ICs and time bases as CPUs, CPU's viewed as an ISA, port a compiler to said ISA, then run an OS on it.

    It teaches you abstraction. More importantly, it teaches you a lot about the right amount of abstraction to use. Not too much, not too little. It teaches you how hardware works and understand the machine. CPUs , GPU, and their ilk leak abstraction everywhere. if you need fast code (which more often than not you really do not) there you need to understand the beasts beneath.

    It also teaches about communication on interfaces. About the right way to design interfaces. Id argue that even when abstraction doesn't leak a well designed interface will make or break your code / device / product whatever.

    Besides being badass, I guess all this served you well.
