    In your "was CLNP really broken" post you state that host-based adressing doesn't scale. Could you explain why that is the case?

  2. Ivan Pepelnjak 12 May 2021 12:14

    With host-based addressing, the routers have to advertise individual host addresses in routing protocols (at least until the first summarization boundary). With subnet-based addressing, they only advertise subnets.

    Not that we couldn't solve that today (after all, we're often using host routing in IP), but 30 years ago it was a big deal.
