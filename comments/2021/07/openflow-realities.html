    OpenFlow was based on the assumption that switches/routers are sealed appliances: hardware and software can't/won't be disaggregated, you can't write your own NOS (with less than $50M funding), you can't customize existing NOSes, etc. That painted them into a corner where the only solution was a centralized external control plane. Disaggregation was announced just a few years later.

    Maybe the lesson to be learned here is to question assumptions thoroughly when they lead you to a bad place.
