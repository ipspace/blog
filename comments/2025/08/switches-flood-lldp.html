    I recall something similar happening with Arista switches at some point, not with LLDP but one of the other slow protocols (can't remember which one).

    Without going into standards (nobody reads them anyway), the way it's usually implemented in a switch is that there is a ACL with a list of special MAC addresses that the switch should capture for further processing and not just flood. If the special MAC address for a given protocol is not on this list, it's treated as a regular Ethernet frame, i.e. flooded.
