    Love the article.

    When I started in 2002 my old boss told me to always hard code the router-id. You never know when the config of an interface, even a loopback, could be changed or deleted.

    Later, I was playing around in the lab and realized you could use 0.0.0.x on Cisco gear for your router-ids. It worked great! When you issue commands like show ip ospf database the router-ids stick out like a road flare. They look distinctly different from IPv4 addresses.

    When I tried doing that on Procurve, no luck! 0.0.0.0/8 was unavailable for router-ids. OK. On that gear I used 1.0.0.x. Nobody uses 1.0.0.0/8... until CloudFlare cleaned it up and started using it. That's another interesting story. Cloudflare has great blogs explaining the engineering in their products.

    I have no idea if you can use 0.x.x.x on Juniper or any other vendor. If someone knows, drop a comment.
