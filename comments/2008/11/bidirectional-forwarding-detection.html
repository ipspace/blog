    BFD is a good enabler for OAM starved protocols (Ethernet), but buys you slightly less for a SONET/SDH transport based IP network. Consider an ultra long haul link and how quickly a SONET component can signal interface down and hence propagate that to the IGP. I say only slightly less, because BFD helps a tad against soft failures. Most people don't monitor their interface counters as religiously as they should. BFD helps safeguard slightly against this. Now, if you take a large scale network where link aggregation is necessary and BFD breaks down instantly because it hashes to one link. Moral is, BFD is helpful but no pancea.
